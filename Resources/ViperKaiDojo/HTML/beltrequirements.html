We Are

VIPER KAI!

As with most martial arts disciplines, the color of the belt you wear in Viper Kai represents your level of skill and training. Wearing a belt is a great honor and source of pride for our students, so you are expected to continually be advancing to higher belt colors, in order to demonstrate your dedication to the Viper Kai way.

Below you will see the specific set of skills required to advance from each of the belts into the next one. Our classes will focus on teaching you these skills, and when your instructor feels you are ready, he will set up a Belt Promotion Ceremony. This is where you will perform your required skills for the whole dojo, and for your invited guests. Successfully passing the Belt Promotion Ceremony is a great accomplishment, and lies at the heart of the Viper Kai mission.

White Belt • Basic Level

• 3 basic kicks • 3 basic hand techniques

• Basic stances • White Belt Form

Yellow Belt • Basic Level

• 3 variation kicks • 2 variation hand techniques

• 3 defensive blocks • Yellow Belt Form

Orange Belt • Basic Level

• 3 offensive kicks • 2 grapple techniques

• New stance • Orange Belt Form

Green Belt • Intermediate Level

• 2 intermediate kicks • 2 spinning kicks

• 2 grapple techniques • Green Belt Form

Blue Belt • Intermediate Level

• 2 intermediate kicks • 1 back spinning kick

• 2 intermediate blocks • Blue Belt Form

Purple Belt • Intermediate Level

• 3 intermediate kicks • 2 spinning kicks

• 1 grapple technique • Purple Belt Form

Brown Belt • Advanced Level

• 3 advanced kicks • 2 jump kicks • 2 advanced hand techniques

• 2 advanced blocks • Lock Flow 1 • Brown Belt Form

Red Belt • Advanced Level

• 2 advanced kicks • 3 jump spinning kicks • 2 advanced grapple techniques

• Lock Flow 2 and 3 • Red Belt Form

Black Belt • Expert Level

• 4 expert kicks • 2 jump spinning kicks • 1 tornado kick • 2 expert hand techniques

• 2 custom takedowns • Brown Belt Form • Red Belt Form

In addition, the following requirements are expected of all Expert Level students on a weekly basis:

• 100 push-ups • 100 sit-ups

• 100 jumping jacks • 1 mile run
