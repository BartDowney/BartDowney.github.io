A little about me...

My journey to this point has been a long and winding one. After graduating from High School in California, I joined the Marine Corps and served six years as an aircraft mechanic, specifically maintaining the ejection seats and personnel environmental systems of various models of A4 Skyhawks.

After my Honorable Discharge, I immediately started a family, which quickly grew to five rambunctious children. Throughout this, I worked whatever jobs I could find, mostly in retail management. As time went on and life took its inevitable turns, I became a single father, raising these children mostly on my own. I stuck with what I knew, spending more years than I had ever planned in the realm of customer service, while becoming something of an expert at communication and conflict resolution.

Eventually, as my children grew up and moved on to continue their own journeys, I was given the opportunity to return to school as a disabled veteran (the result of a back injury suffered during my time of service). So it was that right in the middle of the 2020 Covid lockdown, I found myself back in the classroom, both virtually and in person, and now three years later, I have graduated with honors from Mid-State Technical College.

Today my journey is at another crossroads, with my future looking brighter than ever. I am excited to see what opportunities are out there for me, and I can't wait to get started!
